# Compatibility

## Introduction

PS2-EtherDrive is currently being developed primarily for PlayStation 2 Slim consoles.

Because the project combines custom hardware, embedded networking, internal storage, and modified Ethernet routing, compatibility testing will be an important part of development.

This document tracks currently targeted systems, known limitations, and future testing goals.

---

# Current Development Targets

## Primary Focus

Initial development is focused on later PlayStation 2 Slim models.

These systems are attractive because they do not support traditional internal HDD solutions while still supporting network-based loading methods.

Current target categories include:

- SCPH-7500x
- SCPH-7700x
- SCPH-7900x
- SCPH-9000x

---

# DECKARD Models

## Main Development Focus

A major focus of the project is later DECKARD-based Slim systems.

These systems are especially interesting because they:

- Lack internal HDD expansion capability
- Already rely heavily on network loading methods
- Have compact internal layouts
- Benefit from integrated networking solutions

Compatibility may vary between motherboard revisions.

---

# Earlier Slim Models

## Potential Support

Earlier Slim models may eventually be supported depending on:

- Internal space availability
- Power considerations
- Mechanical fitment
- Ethernet layout differences

Support status for earlier Slim systems remains under evaluation.

---

# PS2 Fat Compatibility

## Not Currently Targeted

PS2 Fat systems are not currently the primary focus of the project.

These systems already support internal HDD solutions and therefore have less need for an integrated networking/storage platform like PS2-EtherDrive.

Future experimentation may still occur.

Testing areas include:

- Internal temperature rise
- RF shield heat transfer
- Long-term stability
- Thermal interaction with existing PS2 components
